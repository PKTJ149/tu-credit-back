# Public Discovery Design Brief v1

## Purpose

This brief translates the public discovery flow and screen spec into a design-ready brief for a future UI design pass.

It is intended for:

- product design
- UI design
- agency handoff
- prototype creation

## Scope

This brief covers:

- Home
- Programs
- Program Detail
- Subjects
- Subject Detail
- Help Center
- About CREDIT BANK
- News and News Detail

This brief does not yet cover:

- authenticated member experiences
- payment flows
- internal administrative content

## Primary Product Goals

1. Explain CREDIT BANK clearly to new visitors.
2. Build institutional trust fast.
3. Make browsing useful before sign-in.
4. Help visitors move from discovery to register/login naturally.
5. Replace content inconsistency and outdated portal behavior with a clearer public story.

## UX Problems This Design Must Solve

- Public pages currently show logged-in controls.
- Program and subject discovery are present but not clearly connected.
- News content currently includes irrelevant template residue.
- Help/manual content is too document-like.

## Audience Mindset

Typical visitor concerns:

- What is CREDIT BANK?
- Is this official and trustworthy?
- What can I study here?
- Do I need an account now or later?
- Which path should I explore first: program or subject?

The design should answer these without making the visitor feel forced into admin-style navigation.

## Design Intent

The public discovery journey should feel:

- clear
- official
- welcoming
- informative
- modern but not flashy

The experience should not feel:

- like an old institutional portal
- blocked too early by authentication
- cluttered with irrelevant operational details

## Suggested Visual Direction

This is a provisional direction aligned with the broader redesign:

- Tone: institutional, contemporary, welcoming
- Layout: clear narrative with strong browsing pathways
- Density: moderate, with strong emphasis on scannability
- Trust signals: university identity, accurate content, clear offering structure
- Interaction feel: guided, informative, low-friction

## Content Priorities By Screen

### Home

Must emphasize:

- what CREDIT BANK is
- why it matters
- how to begin

### Programs

Must emphasize:

- comparison
- filtering
- easy path into program details

### Program Detail

Must emphasize:

- program fit
- requirements
- outcomes
- next action

### Subjects

Must emphasize:

- subject discovery independent of programs
- quick evaluation

### Subject Detail

Must emphasize:

- what the subject is
- credits
- who it is for
- related path forward

### Help Center

Must emphasize:

- task-based guidance
- fast wayfinding

### About CREDIT BANK

Must emphasize:

- institutional trust
- purpose
- legitimacy

### News

Must emphasize:

- verified, relevant updates
- clean separation from the main browse journey

## Structural Recommendations

### Layout

- Home should be narrative and directional, not just a content dump.
- Programs and Subjects should prioritize filtering and scanability.
- Detail pages should be decision-support pages, not brochure pages alone.

### Navigation

- Use public header only.
- Keep register/sign-in accessible but not dominant on every scroll moment.

### Hierarchy

- One primary decision per page.
- Strong path from browse -> detail -> auth.

## Component Priorities

Components that should be designed first:

1. Public header
2. Home hero/value proposition block
3. Program card
4. Subject card
5. Filter/search controls
6. Detail-page summary block
7. Help article/category block
8. Trust/institutional content section

## Responsive Priorities

- Browsing and filtering must remain usable on mobile.
- Card density should stay scannable without becoming shallow or repetitive.
- Detail-page CTA should remain obvious on smaller screens.

## Success Criteria

The design pass is successful if:

- new visitors understand the system quickly
- trust is visibly stronger than on the current site
- programs and subjects are easier to evaluate
- register/login feels like a natural next step, not an arbitrary wall

## Open Questions

- Which discovery path is strategically more important in v1: programs or subjects?
- How much detail should be visible before sign-in?
- Which trust signals matter most to stakeholders and users?
- Should Help Center be surfaced globally or primarily from task dead-ends?
