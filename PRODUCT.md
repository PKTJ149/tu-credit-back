# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Primary users are learners using Thammasat University Credit Bank to access an account, complete onboarding, manage registrations, review payment status, and continue into protected study-related workflows.

Secondary users include university staff, developers, designers, and agency partners who need a clear, maintainable product interface that can be expanded flow by flow.

Users arrive with task intent rather than browsing intent. They need to understand what is required, complete forms confidently, recover from errors, and continue to the next protected action without confusion.

## Product Purpose

Thammasat University Credit Bank helps learners manage flexible learning activity through a clearer digital product experience.

The redesign exists to replace an outdated and inconsistent legacy website with a modern, trustworthy, accessible, and flow-based interface. Success means users can complete account access, onboarding, registration, payment, and credit-transfer tasks with less friction and fewer dead ends.

## Brand Personality

The product should feel official, calm, and supportive.

The interface should communicate institutional credibility without feeling bureaucratic. It should feel contemporary and efficient while staying clear enough for users who may not be technically confident.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic university portal UI that feels dated, dense, and hard to scan.

Avoid startup-style SaaS decoration that weakens institutional trust.

Avoid unrelated legacy wording from prior audit findings, including mismatched provident-fund or non-Credit-Bank support language.

Avoid ornamental UI patterns that distract from tasks: oversized decorative heroes, repeated generic card grids, unnecessary glass effects, gradient text, and purely decorative motion.

## Design Principles

1. Design by complete user flow, not isolated screens.
2. Make every required action obvious, especially forms, validation, payment, registration, and protected-route gates.
3. Preserve institutional trust while reducing the old portal feeling.
4. Keep the component system small, reusable, and consistent across flows.
5. Treat accessibility, responsive behavior, and error states as part of the core product, not polish after the fact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for contrast, focus visibility, keyboard access, semantic structure, and form validation.

The interface should support learners with varied digital confidence levels. Copy should be direct, calm, and plain. Error messages should explain what happened and what to do next.

Motion should be subtle, state-driven, and respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
